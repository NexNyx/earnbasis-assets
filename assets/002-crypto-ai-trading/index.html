Example Workflow: BTCUSDT Day Trade

How a single sequence of prompts turns raw price action into an executable plan:

  1. Scan: “List 3 high-probability BTC setups today with reasons (structure, liquidity, session).”
  2. Confirm: “Given Setup #2, specify exact entry, invalidation, TP1/TP2, and RR ≥ 1:2.”
  3. Stress-test: “If volatility spikes ±1.5σ, how do we adapt SL/TP without breaking RR rules?”
  4. Journal: “Record the trade with entry, exit, reasoning, lesson, and tag by setup type.”

Common Mistakes (and Fixes)

  • Prompt vagueness → Use specific confluence (timeframe, structure term, trigger candle).
  • RR drift → Enforce a minimum RR with an explicit rule in every prompt.
  • No journaling → Use the included template after every trade to build feedback loops.
